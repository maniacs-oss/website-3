--- title: Tools for Remote Workers and Digital Nomads description: Working on a remote team or as a digital nomad can be challenging. Timezones and loneliness are just a couple of the issues you'll face. But luckily, with the right tools you can stay productive and sane as a remote worker.

Here are the tools we use here at Speak, as well as some other nifty tools that our remote working friends recommend. If you find it useful, we encourage you to tweet this page to help spread the word. layout: page ---

  • Workfrom

    Workfrom is a community of people with the shared goal of finding and sharing great places to work remotely all over the world. With filters for things like power outlets, alcohol, and reliable wifi, it’s easy to find a great place to hunker down and get shit done.

    Learn more

  • Remotive

    Remotive is a weekly newsletter that shares interesting tips for remote working as well as job opportunities.

    Learn more

  • Koding

    Koding provides a cloud based IDE where you can collaborate on code with your team mates in real time. Gone are the days of running local VMs and coding in isolation within your local IDE.

    Learn more

  • Zapier

    Use Zapier to automate your web experience and save time throughout your day. Zapier allows you to hook up two apps together to perform task based operations. If one thing happens in App A: trigger something else to happen in App B. It's a fantastic time saver for avoiding repetitive tasks and optimizing your workflow!

    Learn more

  • iDoneThis

    iDoneThis is great tool for keeping up with what your team is working on. Use it to track team tacks and keep track of whatever your team mates have been working on. It’s very simple but incredibly powerful for passive, asynchronous communication.
